Community Reserve

Community Reserve

The Community Reserve is a treasury system where a portion of transaction fees is automatically allocated to fund public goods and ecosystem development.

Files: src/policy/fleetcredits-fees.h, src/fleetcredits.cpp

Purpose

The Community Reserve ensures sustainable development and growth of the Fleet Credits ecosystem by:

  • Funding infrastructure improvements
  • Supporting development and maintenance
  • Sponsoring security audits
  • Funding marketing and community initiatives
  • Supporting educational content and documentation
  • Providing grants for ecosystem projects

Fee Allocation

When transactions pay fees (non-zero fee transactions):

  • Fee Threshold: Only transactions ‚â•1,000 FC pay fees
  • Reserve Portion: A percentage of collected fees goes to the reserve
  • Miner Portion: Remaining fees go to miners
  • Automatic: Allocation happens automatically during block validation

Reserve Management

Multisig Wallet

The reserve is secured by a multisignature wallet:

  • Signers: Multiple trusted community members
  • Threshold: Requires N-of-M signatures for spending
  • Security: Prevents single-point-of-failure
  • Transparency: All reserve transactions are public on-chain

Governance Process

Spending from the reserve requires community governance:

  1. Proposal: Community member submits spending proposal
  2. Discussion: Public discussion period on proposal
  3. Voting: FC holders vote on the proposal
  4. Execution: If approved, multisig signers execute the transaction
  5. Verification: Spending is tracked and auditable on-chain

Reserve Address

The Community Reserve uses a dedicated address:

  • Publicly known address for transparency
  • Funds can be tracked via blockchain explorer
  • Balance visible to all network participants

Spending Examples

Typical uses of the Community Reserve:

  • Development: Paying developers for core improvements
  • Security: Funding security audits and bug bounties
  • Infrastructure: Running nodes, APIs, explorers
  • Education: Creating tutorials, documentation, videos
  • Marketing: Advertising, conferences, community events
  • Grants: Supporting ecosystem projects and contributions

Transparency

All reserve operations are transparent:

  • On-Chain: All transactions visible on blockchain
  • Reporting: Regular reports on reserve balance and spending
  • Proposals: All proposals publicly documented
  • Voting: Voting results publicly recorded

Future Enhancements

Planned improvements to the reserve system:

  • Automated proposal submission via RPC
  • On-chain voting mechanism
  • Reserve balance RPC command
  • Spending history tracking
  • Quarterly reserve reports
Note: The Community Reserve is separate from block rewards. Block rewards go to miners, while transaction fees are split between miners and the reserve. This ensures sustainable funding for ecosystem development while maintaining mining incentives.
